Interests

Career

I have always had a passion for mathematics and after completing my BSc in mathematics I realised that I wanted to help people using mathematics skills I had developed. I initially looked into teaching but realised the level of mathematics was not high enough at secondary school. To this end I have started to develop skills in mathematical modelling, inparticular a MSc in mathematical medicine and biology at Nottingham University. This gave me a good view of many different modelling techniques and introduced me to mathematical and computation coding in both Matlab and Pyton. From there I joined the Mathematics of real world systems centre for doctoral training at the University of Warwick. In the masters year I was introduced to many new topics and techniques. I also met my current supervisor who introduced me to my current field of research, mathematical pharmacology. The current project which I am focusing on is mathematical chrono-pharmacology, which is timing drug delivery to the optimum time of day for the patient. This has lead me to a much deeper understanding of the rhythms of the body and the mathematical techniques that can be used to solve these problems and give clinicians more detail in understanding the way drugs interact with our bodies.
